Let's Get Started!

The Basics

  • Bookmark http://app.teamsupport.com. This is where you will log in each time.

  • Add Team Members

    You can add as many co-workers as you like. This way, you can assign tickets to each other, comment on them and really see TeamSupport in action. If it's just you, that's ok too!

  • Add Customers **Enterprise & Support Desk Editions Only**

    TeamSupport allows you to track tickets against your customers. When a customer submits a ticket either via email or the customer portal, they are auto associated to the ticket. You can also manually associate them to any ticket. This is also how you allow them to access your customer portal (see below under Customer Portals).

  • Create Your First Ticket

    • Email Integration

  • Your system email address:

    You can create tickets from email, forward emails and reply to emails. To test how the email integration works, simply send or forward an email to your system email address. Wait about a minute and you should see a new ticket appear on the Dashboard and under All Tickets.

  • Setup more email options.

  • Email integration plays a big role in your use of TeamSupport. Find out more details.

Customize

  • Setup Ticket Types

    Ticket Types are categories that let you better define the types of tickets you want to manage in your account. Click the expander next to "All Tickets" and you will see some predefined Ticket Types we've made for you. You are welcome to keep these, or you can change them - it's up to you.

  • Add custom fields to your tickets and other places

    Adding custom fields allows you to better define the properties in your account. While we have many stock fields, we know you may have more data you want to capture and track. You can add as many custom fields as you like.

  • Modify the ticket statuses

    Ticket statuses allow you to define the ticket lifecycyle. They are also unique to each Ticket Type. Since different types of tickets may be dealt with differently in your business, this makes it very easy to track what is going on across the board.

  • Modify status workflow

    Workflow allows you to determine which ticket statuses can be selected based on the current status of a ticket. For example, you may want to limit someone trying to go from Step1 to Step3 without going to Step2 first.

Customer Portals

  • Set up your Portals

    There are two main portal types in TeamSupport. The Advanced Portal and the Public Portal.

    **Please note: the Advanced Portal is only available in our Enterprise and Support Desk Editions. If you have signed up for Express and would like to change editions, please email support@teamsupport.com and a member of our team will help you.**

    All portals are designed to fit within your website via an iframe. This allows you to keep your customers within your website and not redirect them some where else. See this in action now on our site.

    • The Advanced Portal is secure and requires your customers to login - although we do support the concept of single sign on (assuming they have already logged into your site/app). The portal let's them submit tickets, view/comment on existing tickets, search the KB and also join in the community forum conversations .
    • The Public Portal is non-authinticated and also does not include the community/forum ability, but your customers can submit tickets, look up and comment on their open tickets and search the KB.

    You can read our online help documents regarding the portals here.

Chat

  • Try out chat here

    All accounts include live customer chat. When you are online, your customers can chat with you and your team. When you are offline, your customer chats are immediately converted into tickets for you to respond to later. You can customize your "online" and "offline" buttons and also brand the chat window the customer sees. Learn how to setup chat.
